Machine Learning aided design of materials with target elastic properties

The construction of crystal feature vector is crucial to machine learning (ML) technique applied in materials science. Here, we present a set of universal descriptors which combines atomic properties with crystal fingerprint to build interpretable models for elastic property prediction. This approach is demonstrated powerful to the prediction of elastic moduli with minor deviations with respect to density functional theory (DFT) based calculations. Besides, we develop an effective method to evaluate the influence of each descriptor, and find bond strength related properties are most important, which indicates that the ML model captures the underlying physics of the bulk and shear moduli. Moreover, the projection analysis shows data points with large and small elastic moduli are clearly divided into two parts by the average value of volume and atomization enthalpy (∆Hatomic), suggesting that compounds composed elements with large ∆Hatomic such as V, Pt, Zr, Hf, Ta, Mo, W, Tc, Re, Ru, Os, Ir and non-metallic elements B, C, N, Si, are potential large elastic moduli materials. Following this rule, we design a new dynamic stable material ReTcB4 which has higher shear modulus than any unexplored materials in the database. This method is valuable for high throughout screening and materials design.

Research Fields

Low Dimensional Materials

We are interested in low dimensional materials, including their electronic properties,magnectic properties and superconductivity.These materials have found use in applications such as the next generation of nanoscale semiconductor devices, solar battery, and chemical catalyst.Recently,the study of graphene-like materials such as silicene, germanene,borophene,and monolayer boron-carbon etc have aroused great interest among researchers in the field of condensed matter physics.

Nanostructures

With the first-principle calculations ,We have the ability to study the electronic properties of nanostructures, the metastable state of growth processes and their dynamics of the growth process.

Machine Learning

Density functional theory(DFT) is a mature technique for calculating the structure and behavior of solids,which enable the development of extensive databases that cover the calculated properties of known and hypothetical systems,including organic and inorgannic crystals,single molecules and metal alloys. Given enough data and a rule-discovery algorithm, a computer has the potential to determine all known physical laws without human input.The combination of big data and machine-learning approaches give researchers the ability to predict materials' properties ,discover new materials,even establish new scientific laws and principles.
